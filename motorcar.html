Motor Car Overview

Definition

A motor car is a self-propelled land vehicle with wheels that is used to transport people or cargo.

History

The first steam-powered road vehicle was built in 1769 by Nicolas-Joseph Cugnot, a French inventor. The first internal combustion-powered automobile was designed by Fran√ßois Isaac de Rivaz in 1808. The modern car was invented in 1886 by German inventor Carl Benz.

Features

Cars have a variety of controls for driving, parking, passenger comfort, and lighting. Modern cars also have features like air conditioning, navigation systems, and in-car entertainment.

Fuel

Cars run on fuels like petrol, diesel, and CNG. Electrically charged cars have also become popular.

Safety

Driving in a car is safer than riding in a fixed-wing aircraft. However, driving without proper experience can lead to fatal road accidents.

Environmental Impact

Burning gasoline as fuel pollutes the atmosphere. Cars are equipped with a catalytic converter that reduces pollution by converting harmful gases into carbon dioxide and water.
